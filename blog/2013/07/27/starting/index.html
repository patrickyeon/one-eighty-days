Starting

Jul 27th, 2013

180 Releases, 180 Days. Getting started.

Jennifer Dewalt is building 180 websites in 180 days, and absolutely killing it. Derek Sivers has a good comment on producing work as an artist on the related Hacker News page. It’s a recurring theme in Sivers’ blog posts: you need to get out and ship. Seth Godin says it a lot too: “Real artists ship”.

Jennifer’s powering away on that by blocking off 10 hours a day to produce a new idea and learn everything it takes her to pull it off. I won’t commit to 10 hours a day, but I do want to do something similar. So here’s my version:

One hundred and eighty days. One release a day. No days off.

A “release” is a completely subjective measurement, I’ll just say “I’ll know it when I see it”. I’m not promising to create something new each day, in fact my initial drive is to ship a number of small projects I’ve dicked around with and keep on telling myself “I’ll just clean this up later and then put it online”. But any release will be something I haven’t put out there before. I’m making my own set of rules:

  • Every release is available for free (as in beer) and linked to with a blog post. (Furthermore, I expect the majority of my stuff will be released under a free software or creative commons-like license.)
  • Every release will be the product of some respectable amount of work. One-liners, simple photos, and quickly-written posts are not enough.
  • If I don’t release something and publish a blog post between 12:01 AM and 11:59 PM (PST), that day’s missed and I start over at day 1.
  • I can quit when I’ve successfully hit 180 days in a row.

These rules are more flexible than Jennifer’s and I’m not restricting myself to one medium, but again she’s beasting through this and I’m just trying to spur myself into a little more action. I trust that I’ll be hard enough on myself to keep the bar high enough and not phone it in any days just to keep a streak going.

Today is day one. This is not the first release, but the next post will be.
